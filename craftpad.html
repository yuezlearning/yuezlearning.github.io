CraftPad: Enabling Human-AI Collaboration in Lesson Design

An AI-powered educational tool that helps teachers generate structured, high-quality lesson designs aligned with pedagogical theories — saving time while supporting effective teaching practices.

Role

Product Designer, AI Engineer

Duration

02/2026-11/2026

Stack

Generative AI, RAG, Python, JavaScript

01Overview

Teachers spend a significant amount of time creating lesson plans, often struggling to maintain consistent quality across courses. CraftPad was designed as an AI-powered educational tool to streamline this process.

It helps teachers generate structured, high-quality lesson designs aligned with pedagogical theories — saving time while supporting effective teaching practices.

Smoother workflow

03Problem

Through research and teacher interviews, we identified three major challenges:

01

Time Intensive

Creating lesson designs consumes a large portion of teachers' daily workload.

02

Inconsistent Quality

Quality varies depending on individual expertise, energy, and available resources.

03

Lack of Pedagogical Alignment

Lesson designs often lack systematic adherence to established teaching theories.

04Ideation

We explored ways to make the process more intuitive and flexible:

Refined Workflow

Teachers provide basic lesson details → AI generates → teachers review and refine → finalize and evaluate.

Whiteboard Format

A flexible, visual whiteboard interface that mirrors teachers' natural editing and organizing habits.

05Design

AI-Driven Design Decisions

1. Structured Input

Simplified input fields guide teachers unfamiliar with prompt engineering to provide the right context for AI generation.

2. Modular Output

Lesson designs are broken down into sections (introduction, activities, assessment) so teachers can refine parts individually.

3. Teacher Control

Multiple ways to edit — section-by-section feedback, manual editing, or regenerating the entire plan — ensure teachers remain in control.

4. Ethical Safeguards with RAG

Integrated Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) ensures outputs are grounded in a vetted knowledge base of pedagogical theories, minimizing bias and hallucination.

06Reflection

Designing for human-AI collaboration

The biggest challenge was striking the right balance between AI automation and teacher agency. Too much AI makes teachers feel replaceable; too little defeats the purpose. We learned that framing AI as a "first draft generator" — not a decision-maker — earned teacher trust.

Prompt engineering as a design skill

Working on the AI backend taught me that prompt design is UX design — structuring inputs, anticipating edge cases, and shaping outputs for clarity are the same skills applied in a different medium.

Cross-functional communication

Bridging the gap between pedagogical researchers who think in frameworks and engineers who think in systems was a constant negotiation. I learned to translate between both languages, which made the final product stronger.
