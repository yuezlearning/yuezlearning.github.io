MuseoGo: Making Museum Learning Effective and Engaging

A mobile app facilitating learning for children at museums by integrating human-AI collaboration. Gained 95% user satisfaction and presented at two conferences.

Product Designer

Duration

01Overview

Museums have great potential for fostering curiosity and learning, but traditional visits often lead to passive experiences and missed opportunities for deeper engagement. MuseoGo addresses these gaps by offering an AI-driven, three-phase support system:

Before visit

Personalized itinerary plan

During visit

Real-time interactive chatbot

After visit

Exit ticket and review

03Problem

Picture this: A family enters a world-class museum. Within 30 minutes, the kids are bored, parents are frustrated, and everyone's ready to leave. Sound familiar?

Despite museums investing $2 billion annually in educational resources, the average engagement rate hovers at just 46%. We discovered a startling disconnect: while museums offer incredible learning opportunities, families struggle to access and utilize them effectively.

This led us to ask: Why is engagement so low, and how might we make museum visits more interactive and effective for children?

04Research

Through interviews with 6 families and 2 museum educators, we uncovered three critical moments where learning breaks down:

Parent

"Managing my kids in a large museum can be stressful. I wish there were tools that keep them excited and learning on their own."

Children

"I don't know where to start. Sometimes I don't understand what the exhibit is about. After a while, I became bored."

Museum Educator

"The most difficult part is keeping children's attention. They forget most of what they learned after leaving."

Key Pain Points

01

Pre-Visit: Families arrive unprepared, overwhelmed by choices

02

During Visit: Children become passive observers, not active learners

03

After Visit: The experience ends at the exit — no reflection, no continued exploration

05Ideation

Our proposed solution was to create a personal museum guide for children — a tool that supports them before, during, and after their visit, keeping curiosity alive at every stage.

Core features include personalized itineraries, preview podcasts, real-time Q&A with quizzes, exit tickets, AI-generated review songs, and curated follow-up resources.

We shifted from an iPad prototype to a mobile-first design. At the center is Museo, a playful character designed to guide children through their journey.

06Design

Design System

I built the design system from the ground up. Bright orange as the primary color conveys energy and excitement, paired with Poppins — a rounded sans-serif that feels playful and approachable for children.

Design Decisions

We conducted two rounds of usability testing, iteratively refining prototypes to improve clarity, engagement, and overall usability.

1. Form Design

Streamlined into step-by-step sections to reduce cognitive load, with progress indicators, optional questions, and a final review page.

2. AI-generated Itinerary

Three iterations: V1 text-heavy; V2 simplified with steps; V3 added navigation icons, time estimates, and directions.

3. Accessibility

Alternative AI guide options for young children, age-appropriate tone, and multilingual support.

4. Minimalist Exit Ticket

Transformed from a physical format into a digitally friendly timeline — clean and visually clear for reflection.

07Solutions

We reimagined the entire museum journey through the lens of a child's curiosity:

Pre-Visit: Building Excitement

  • • AI-Powered Planning: Families answer fun questions about interests and learning styles
  • • Personalized Itinerary: AI creates a custom adventure path
  • • Preview Podcast: Kids listen to an engaging story about what they'll discover

During Visit: Active Exploration

  • • AI Knowledge Buddy: Age-appropriate answers to any question
  • • Interactive Challenges: Location-based quizzes and family activities
  • • Voice Input: Speak naturally, removing reading barriers

After Visit: Extending the Journey

  • • Digital Exit Ticket: A personalized recap of their adventure
  • • AI-Generated Song: Learnings transformed into a catchy tune
  • • Curated Resources: Follow-up activities based on interest

08Reflection

Designing AI with humanity

To make the experience feel more like a friendship than a chatbot, we gave Museo a warm personality: greeting children at the start, accompanying them throughout, and even "writing" a personalized song as a gift.

Defining the right audience

Initially aimed at parents, research revealed they see museums as playtime. We pivoted to focus on children as primary users, giving them a learning companion in Museo.

Growing technical skills

I learned AI prompt engineering and explored generative AI, which informed realistic design decisions and strengthened collaboration with engineers.
